About

We are the official Enactus team at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. Founded in 2007, our organization exists to create real change through our projects while preparing our members to succeed in their future careers.



Our Mission

We are a community of student, academic, and business leaders committed to using the power of entrepreneurial action to transform lives and shape a better, more sustainable world.

Projects

Our projects are the heart of what we do. Typically, a project will be sourced from our members. New members are assigned to projects based on preference and project needs. Each of these projects have been carefully sourced, analyzed, and supported to ensure their impact and success.

Grounds for Growth

Grounds for Growth upcycles discarded soap and coffee grounds to create sustainably sourced soap and scrub products in cooperation with the residents of Restoration Urban Ministries.

Stay Glassy

Stay Glassy operates to reduce glass waste and provide employment opportunities locally.Through selling a product line of candles, drinking glasses, and decorative plants online and at community events, Stay Glassy conducts upcycling education.

Circle Cycle

Circle Cycle is promoting bicycles as a vehicle for social change to empower local youth and increase mobility. Circle Cycle is creating a year-round “Earn a Bike” program utilizing abandoned bicycles at our local Boys and Girls Club.

Donable

Donable facilitates a comprehensive network between local nonprofits and their local communities through an online platform to simplify donations.

Justice for Juveniles

Justice for Juveniles aims to reduce youth recidivism in the Urbana-Champaign community. Justice for Juveniles is creating an entrepreneurship program for youth aged 16-24 in collaboration with the founder of DREAAM (Driven to Reach Academic Achievement for Males) House.

Tire-Less

Tire-Less is mitigating tire waste in the local community through developing durable and eco-friendly products while raising attention about proper disposal methods for tires.

Project Phoenix

Phoenix will work to teach applicable business skills to high school students through their passions in graphic design. In addition, Phoenix aims to empower students with a platform to spread a message about social issues.

Together, Youth & Elderly (T.Y.E)

T.Y.E (Together, Youth, and Elderly) aims to address the issue of elderly loneliness. T.Y.E is facilitating activities between youth and elderly that promote artistic and entrepreneurial collaboration.

Research and Development

Research and Development focuses on product innovation around environmental or social needs, independent of our existing projects.

Our Journey

Founded in 2007, our organization exists to create real change through our projects while preparing our members to succeed in their future careers. With a head for business and a heart for the world, we live our values of integrity, passion, innovation and collaboration. Enactus is the world’s largest experiential learning platform dedicated to creating a better world while developing the next generation of entrepreneurial leaders and social innovators.

  • 2007

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Illinois Enactus is founded as a chapter of SIFE - Students in Free Enterprise. SIFE was the world's most successful program helping university students to create community empowerment projects

  • 2012

    Rebranding: Enactus

    SIFE changes its name to Enactus - (En)trepreneurial (Act)ion (Us) - to reaffirm its long-standing commitment to using entrepreneurial action as a catalyst for progress.

  • 2012-2013

    Regional Champions

    Illinois Enactus are Regional Champions in both the 2012 and 2013 Enactus USA Regional Competition.

  • 2014-2015

    National Quarterfinalists

    Illinois Enactus are National Quarterfinalists in both the 2014 and 2015 Enactus USA National Competition.

  • 2016

    Illinois Enactus reaches the Top 12

    At the 2016 Enactus USA National Competition held in St Louis, Missouri, Illinois Enactus places 3rd in the Semi-Final Round, our best result yet.

  • 2017

    Illinois Enactus places Top 8

    Illinois Enactus achieves a Top 8 showing in the 2017 Enactus USA National Competition. We presented our projects Grounds for Growth, En-ABLE-ing Entrepreneurs, and Operation Forward.

Why join Enactus?

Ready to join the best in the business?

Enactus is unique because it is made up of students from every major imaginable. We need all skill sets to do what we do! As part of Enactus, you will work with a diverse group of students from which you will learn more than you could ever imagine. The field of social entrepreneurship is filled with some of the brightest minds in the world. The partnerships we make through our projects connect us to activist, business, and political leaders across the world. We are all united by common causes and work to eliminate inequities across the globe.

As part of Enactus, you will be part of a family. This family extends far beyond Illinois Enactus. You're joining the largest business network in the world. This network consists of only the most driven students and business leaders. Surrounding yourself with these people is the best way to grow as an individual. On campus, you will be working with and learning from outstanding students and companies. You will have a new group of friends and countless social events to attend. You will also develop a strong bond with your new member class (especially during the New Member Retreat)! The friendships developed in this organization are a large part of what makes the Enactus experience so rewarding.

This organization would not exist without the projects. An Enactus project strives to improve the quality of life and standard of living for the people in need all over the world and in our local community. These projects are more than just community service - we are creating sustainable solutions and empowering individuals. The feeling of achievement you will experience after applying the knowledge you've gained in the classroom and equipping those in need with sustainable solutions you developed is almost impossible to describe. Yes, these projects also help you prepare for your future career, but they will make a much bigger impact than that. They will change the lives of others and shape your world view. By working on diverse teams and developing unique skills, you will gain experience from day one that you can talk about in interviews and for the rest of your life. In Enactus, every member is actively involved in a project, so you will be able to speak on and remember the specific impact you create in someone's life.

Every member of Enactus gains the professional skills needed to succeed after graduation. Your professional development begins through our mentorship program and our numerous workshops and trainings. Our organization will provide you with all the resources you need to write a perfect resume, nail every interview, and navigate the job market. Enactus will hone your ability to receive the internships or full-time positions of your dreams. Enactus has over 200 corporate sponsors and a world-wide network. Career fairs are hosted exclusively for Enactus and several companies that do not actively recruit at UIUC reach out to Enactus alone to recruit interested students. As a member of Enactus, you will attend leadership conferences and interview for positions available only to Enactus members. With all these amazing resources, there are countless opportunities for you to benefit from our professionalism.

Grounds for Growth

Grounds for Growth upcycles discarded soap and coffee grounds to create sustainably sourced soap and scrub products in cooperation with the residents of Restoration Urban Ministries.

Grounds for Growth is a product-based entrepreneurial venture. Currently, it works to produce personal care products such as body soaps and scrubs. We take recycled soaps from the iHotel and combine it with coffee grounds from local shops like Espresso Royale. We also work closely with Restoration Urban Ministries to provide educational lessons to their residents every Friday morning from 10am-12pm.

  • Launched: Spring 2015
  • Project Managers: Sarah Lewand and Lauren Komrska
  • Executive Manager: Josh Gitogo

Stay Glassy

Stay Glassy operates to reduce glass waste and provide employment opportunities locally.

Stay Glassy is a project seeking to upcycle used glass bottles to create an assortment of usable products including vases, candles, drinking glasses, and decorative goods. By taking old glass bottles produced from community waste, our mission is to prevent large amounts of glass from ending up in landfills through our upcycling process. Moreover, we hope our project will continue to promote a source of enjoyment and education for individuals with disabilities through our partnership with the Developmental Services Center (DSC).

  • Launched: Fall 2016
  • Project Manager: Aarthi Mahesh
  • Executive Manager: Josh Gitogo

Circle Cycle

Circle Cycle is promoting bicycles as a vehicle for social change to empower local youth and increase mobility.

Circle Cycle aims to create a community bike shop/bicycle repair program to empower economically disadvantaged youth with technical skills and entrepreneurial skills while providing them a safe space for personal development, while simultaneously addressing the bike waste problem on campus. Circle Cycle placed second in the Ford Mobility Innovation Challenge and was awarded $10,000. Circle Cycle partners with the Don Moyer Boys and Girls Club. The project has received support from Urbana Park District and Champaign Public Library to host interactive one-day events and successfully organized a workshop with the Champaign Public Library last semester.

  • Launched: Fall 2017
  • Project Manager: Jessica Huang
  • Executive Manager: Yoga Sundar

Donable

Donable connects passionate students to non-profits who require resources and student talent to complete projects.

Donable is a project that bridges the gap between non-profits and the Champaign-Urbana community. In previous semesters, Donable focused on facilitating the donation of used goods to non-profits through creating a donation portal. In Spring 2019, Donable conducted an exhaustive survey which revealed a more crucial need - a skilled volunteer platform to connect University students with local non-profits through project-based collaboration models.

  • Launched: Spring 2017
  • Project Manager: Henry Yu
  • Executive Manager: Sydney Kwan

Justice for Juveniles

Justice for Juveniles is creating an entrepreneurship program for youth aged 16-24 in collaboration with the founder of DREAAM (Driven to Reach Academic Achievement for Males) House.

Justice for Juveniles aims to provide economic opportunity to individuals impacted by incarceration through personal development and learning about business development.

  • Launched: Spring 2019
  • Project Manager: Isra Rahman

Tire-Less

Tire-Less is mitigating tire waste in the local community through developing durable and eco-friendly products while raising attention about proper disposal methods for tires.

Tireless is an early-stage project that aims to decrease bike tire waste through upcycling discarded tires into usable, functional products. The project is currently focusing on developing a line of belts and wallets with bike tires that are donated from local bike repair shops. In the long-term, Tireless aims to create a culture of upcycling and reduce waste in the community.

  • Launched: Spring 2019
  • Project Manager: Muskaan Sawhney
  • Executive Manager: Sydney Kwan

Project Phoenix

Phoenix brings applicable business skills to high school students through their passions in graphic design.

Phoenix is a project that addresses the lack of funding for creative subjects in high schools by providing opportunities for students to learn different artistic mediums. Phoenix's first program will be a clothing brand to increase awareness of a social issue or topic. The business operates with the cooperation of local high school students in the Urbana-Champaign area, with the guidance of Enactus members and project partners.

  • Launched: Fall 2019
  • Project Manager: Johnny Fetscher
  • Executive Manager: Josh Gitogo

Project T.Y.E.

T.Y.E (Together, Youth, and Elderly) aims to address the issue of elderly loneliness. T.Y.E is facilitating activities between youth and elderly that promote artistic and entrepreneurial collaboration.

T.Y.E is working towards increasing the quality of life for senior citizens. Those in nursing homes often struggle with neglect, isolation, and many other issues. With Baby Boomers entering old age, there is an increasing need for senior-centric programming. T.Y.E is looking to work with the Urbana-Champaign community to help alleviate some of the issues that come with the current senior-living situation.

  • Launched: Fall 2019
  • Project Manager: Rachel Rosa
  • Executive Manager: Sydney Kwan

Research and Development

Research and Development focuses on product innovation around environmental or social needs, independent of our existing projects.

The Research & Development Team is a project designed to leverage the engineering skills and technological interest of Illinois Enactus members in order to create a technologically innovative solution that solves a societal or environmental problem. As the name implies, this project will be heavily focused on developing a product or service at the intersection of science in the field of interest and customer/user experiences.

  • Launched: Fall 2019
  • Project Manager: Randy Janson
  • Executive Manager: Josh Gitogo

The Recruitment Process

Recruitment takes place at the beginning of each fall and spring semester. Illinois Enactus accepts new members into Project Associate positions. We accept applications from current, graduate, and exchange students at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign.

  1. Attend Information Night (Not Required)
  2. Apply
  3. First-Round Interviews (Invite Only)
  4. Social Night (Invite Only)
  5. Final-Round Interviews (Invite Only)
  6. Invitation To Join

Information Night

DATE at TIME in LOCATION. Doors Open at TIME

Attire: Business Casual

All majors are welcome! Join us at Information Night to learn about our organization. You'll have a chance to hear directly from our members about how Enactus has changed so many lives and how it can change yours too. Bring any questions you have! Be sure to let us know at membership@illinoisenactus.com if you are unable to attend but still wish to apply. You are not required to attend Information Night in order to apply.

Enactus Application Due

DATE AND TIME

You are not required to have been at Information Night in order to apply. The application will ask you for your basic information, a few essay questions, and your resume.

First-Round Interviews

Behavioural Interview

DATE AND TIME

Attire: Business Casual

Selected candidates will be invited to participate in three short behavioral interviews. This is your chance to distinguish yourself as a good fit for Enactus. Be sure to discuss your past experiences that provide us with insight as to whether you will be successful in Enactus.

Social Stage

DATE AND TIME

Attire: Campus Casual

Successful first round candidates will be invited for the social stage on the next day. Rather than itching in a suit or tie, you'll get to know a little more about our members on a personal level. We'll even provide snacks!

Final Round Interviews

DATE AND TIME

Attire: Business Professional

Invited candidates will be asked to work in groups of four to complete a mini case. We will present you with a project-based problem and ask you to devise a creative yet practical solution. Remember, this is your time to shine as a team player and show how you would perform on an Enactus team.
