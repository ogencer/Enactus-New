Our Journey

Founded in 2007, our organization exists to create real change through our projects while preparing our members to succeed in their future careers. With a head for business and a heart for the world, we live our values of integrity, passion, innovation and collaboration. Enactus is the world’s largest experiential learning platform dedicated to creating a better world while developing the next generation of entrepreneurial leaders and social innovators.

  • 2007

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Illinois Enactus is founded as a chapter of SIFE - Students in Free Enterprise. SIFE was the world's most successful program helping university students to create community empowerment projects

  • 2012

    Rebranding: Enactus

    SIFE changes its name to Enactus - (En)trepreneurial (Act)ion (Us) - to reaffirm its long-standing commitment to using entrepreneurial action as a catalyst for progress.

  • 2012-2013

    Regional Champions

    Illinois Enactus are Regional Champions in both the 2012 and 2013 Enactus USA Regional Competition.

  • 2014-2015

    National Quarterfinalists

    Illinois Enactus are National Quarterfinalists in both the 2014 and 2015 Enactus USA National Competition.

  • 2016

    Illinois Enactus reaches the Top 12

    At the 2016 Enactus USA National Competition held in St Louis, Missouri, Illinois Enactus places 3rd in the Semi-Final Round, our best result yet.

  • 2017

    Illinois Enactus places Top 8

    Illinois Enactus achieves a Top 8 showing in the 2017 Enactus USA National Competition. We presented our projects Grounds for Growth, En-ABLE-ing Entrepreneurs, and Operation Forward.

Grounds for Growth

Grounds for Growth is a social entrepreneurship project that enables sustainable progress in the Champaign-Urbana community by up-cycling discarded coffee grounds and vegetable oil into hand-made soap and scrubs.

Grounds for Growth has taught entrepreneurial skills to over 150 residents at Restoration Urban Ministries, a transitional housing program in Champaign. These skills include resume building, goal setting, business communication, and budgeting. Through our soap and scrub production, we upcycled upwards of 50 pounds of coffee waste and hundreds of bars of used soap. This semester, we are working towards an enhanced marketing strategy to drive sales in hopes of overcoming the pandemic’s effect of lower consumer leisure spending. We are also revamping the curriculum to teach business acumen directed towards business operations, in preparation of delinking this project to RUM in the coming years. In addition, we hope to increase our partnerships in the Champaign-Urbana area with local businesses to localize our supply chain and ensure its sustainability.

  • Launched: Spring 2015
  • Project Managers: Hiba Ahmed
  • Executive Manager: Annie Chan

Stay Glassy

Our glass products are not only up-cycled, helping reduce glass waster in the Champaign-Urbana community, but they are handcrafted by W, an individual in the Champaign-Urbana community with an intellectual disability.

Stay Glassy is able to utilize unused wine bottles from a local winery and distribute them as beautiful, customized glass products across campuses at places like Just Bee Acai. With low input costs, we are able to provide W, a member at the Developmental Services Center of Champaign with a source of income and a steady job. For this semester, we plan to focus on outreach and locking down more partnerships, as well as ensuring our partnership with W in order to begin the de-linking process.

  • Launched: Fall 2016
  • Project Managers: Abby Koss & Chaya Lee-Isranukul
  • Executive Manager: Randy Janson

Circle Cycle

Circle Cycle is promoting bicycles as a vehicle for social change to empower local youth and increase mobility through a year-round earn-a-bike program while also addressing the issue of abandoned bicycles on campus.

Circle Cycle partners with local groups to hold six-week earn-a-bike programs where students in the Champaign-Urbana area learn to fix selected bikes and get to take them home at the end. The bicycles used in this program are abandoned bikes obtained from the University Police Department. Since many students abandon their bikes on campus over the summer or after graduation, the bikes have been problematic for the campus environment and a burden for their collection as well as storage. Thus, part of Circle Cycle’s mission is to upcycle those bikes to empower less privileged children with the mobility they deserve. Talk about two birds with one stone! This past semester, Circle Cycle held the first class of the pilot program with 8 students at the Don Moyer Boys and Girls Club. Unfortunately, due to the pandemic, the program was halted and is expected to pick back up in Spring 2021. This semester, Circle Cycle will be focusing on 3 main areas: creating new contacts and partnerships within the CU bike and school community; planning a community-wide bike-a-thon event to raise funds in order to financially support project expenses; and improving upon project structure and curriculum based on observations from last semester. Work in these areas will make sure that we are ready to hit the ground running in the spring when we are able to continue our program!

  • Launched: Fall 2017
  • Project Managers: Andy Wong & Shreya Balaji
  • Executive Manager: Annie Chan

CreAlgae

CreAlgae is focused on creating a petroleum-free algae-based bioplastic using harmful algal blooms from local waterways and chitosan, a waste product from the seafood industry.

At this point in time, CreAlgae has developed a base formula for our bioplastic, and we have numerous prototypes for our product, created using both wild and processed algae. Our future source of algae will primarily be local harmful algal blooms, and we’ve been in frequent contact with local lakes and state parks to confirm the existence of algae around us. CreAlgae is currently focusing on finalizing a formula for our product and conducting market research to identify various applications for our bioplastic. This semester, we will apply to different accelerators and competitions, such as iVenture and the Biodesign Challenge (which we will register for alongside Symmetry). Simultaneously, we will continue to build our advisor network on campus and off. This fall we will also create project branding through the completed design of a logo and published website. These plans will allow CreAlgae to become more established and expand our faculty and corporate resources. Our project is still in its formative stages, and this will be an amazing semester for its growth!

  • Launched: Spring 2020
  • Project Managers: Shreya Balaji & Tara Entezar
  • Executive Manager: Muskaan Sawhney

Symmetry

Making wood without cutting down trees to combat deforestation and empower designers with more sustainable materials.

While Symmetry is one of the newest projects, it has been able to (1) create various colors of 'alternative woods' that resemble many of the exotic, tree-based woods driving deforestation, (2) receive positive, detailed feedback from potential early adopters, or wood hobbyists, in the Urbana-Champaign area, and (3) significantly speed up its manufacturing process. Before it was part of the Enactus portfolio, the concept behind Symmetry was also named as a finalist idea for the Technology Entrepreneur Center's (TEC) 2020 Illinois Innovation Prize. Now, along with CreAlgae, it is looking to represent U of I in the school's first-ever entry in The Biodesign Challenge (BDC), an annual competition in which teams of artists and scientists from universities across the world develop sustainable products with biotechnology. To prepare for this during the next semester, Symmetry is looking to sell small quantities of its material to early adopters, begin research into biotech that can further accelerate its manufacturing, secure partnerships with vendors on campus that can provide the food waste used to grow the alternative wood, and start organizing a coalition of companies and other groups addressing deforestation.

  • Launched: Spring 2019
  • Project Manager: Gabe Tavas
  • Executive Manager: Muskaan Sawhney

Phoenix

Phoenix is an initiative to collaborate with high school students in the community and give them a platform to highlight their fashion design interests.

This previous year, Phoenix recruited many students from the CU community and began workshops and lessons resulting in numerous products being designed and up cycled for sale. During the fall, Phoenix will be focused on an official brand launch, developing research on marketing through social media and brand image advertising, trademarking Phoenix as a public label and functioning e-commerce, and project expansion throughout the Chicago area. We plan to work towards further strengthening student relationships and virtual recruitment in the Fall, working on teaching basic fashion design skills to project associates through a crash course to expand our abilities to interact and help build towards creating new products, and uploading and introducing to the public the fully established Phoenix shopping site. We plan to work on marketing the brand out to the target community and collaborating with our newly established Phoenix board in regards to expansion to organizations and workshops in Chicago. Our goal is to create a foundation to promote sustainability and student’s artistic passions through Phoenix.

  • Launched: Fall 2019
  • Project Manager: Alisha Alam & Sydney Nguyen
  • Executive Manager: Muskaan Sawhney

Sage

Sage works to create intergenerational cohesion between the seniors and youth population through preserving lived experiences through the written word.

Sage has formed community partners with assisted living facilities in which volunteers interact with the residents and interview them on a range of topics. In the first year, we had collected over 20 stories and met with 5 seniors regularly. With these interactions, the residents reported feeling more connected with the youth culture and youth participants reported an increased sense of appreciation for their elders. Going forward, Sage is working to curate custom books that comprise the stories collected from the seniors. With the increased vulnerability of older adults due to COVID-19, Sage is working to integrate the interactions onto a virtual atmosphere.

  • Launched: Fall 2019
  • Project Manager: Katherine Carney
  • Executive Manager: Randy Janson

Hydrologic

Curbing excessive water consumption within the community by implementing low-flow showerheads.

Hydrologic has established partnerships with key campus stakeholders like University Housing and Campus Recreation in order to conduct tests, and potentially implement low-flow showerheads in dorms and gyms across campus. Through outreach, we have also solidified on a needs analysis and intend to use our findings to further the project next semester. We plan to inform our decision to pursue a novel concept vs an existing solution by forming technical partners and collecting data through market research. Furthermore, the team will strive to apply for grants to ease the financial burden of testing showerhead solutions to determine the most appropriate fit for the university. By the end of the semester, Hydrologic aims to decide the avenue for low-flow technology, finalize a university-targeted proposal, and gain technical partnerships.

  • Launched: Fall 2019
  • Project Manager: Vihaan Kalaria & Graham Louthan
  • Executive Manager: Randy Janson
