Projects

Our projects are the heart of what we do. Typically, a project will be sourced from our members. New members are assigned to projects based on preference and project needs. Each of these projects have been carefully sourced, analyzed, and supported to ensure their impact and success.

Grounds for Growth

Grounds for Growth upcycles discarded soap and coffee grounds to create sustainably sourced soap and scrub products in cooperation with the residents of Restoration Urban Ministries.

Stay Glassy

Stay Glassy operates to reduce glass waste and provide employment opportunities locally.Through selling a product line of candles, drinking glasses, and decorative plants online and at community events, Stay Glassy conducts upcycling education.

Circle Cycle

Launched: Fall 2018

Circle Cycle is promoting bicycles as a vehicle for social change to empower local youth and increase mobility. Circle Cycle is creating a year-round “Earn a Bike” program utilizing abandoned bicycles at our local Boys and Girls Club.

Donable

Launched: Spring 2018

Donable facilitates a comprehensive network between local nonprofits and their local communities through an online platform to simplify donations.

Justice for Juveniles

Launched: Spring 2019

Justice for Juveniles aims to reduce youth recidivism in the Champaign-Urbana community. Justice for Juveniles is creating an entrepreneurship program for youth aged 16-24 in collaboration with the founder of DREAAM (Driven to Reach Academic Achievement for Males) House.

Tire-Less

Launched: Spring 2019

Tire-Less is mitigating tire waste in the local community through developing durable and eco-friendly products while raising attention about proper disposal methods for tires.

Project Phoenix

Launched: Fall 2019

Phoenix will work to teach applicable business skills to high school students through their passions in graphic design. In addition, Phoenix aims to empower students with a platform to spread a message about social issues.

Together, Youth & Elderly (T.Y.E)

Launched: Fall 2019

T.Y.E (Together, Youth, and Elderly) aims to address the issue of elderly loneliness. T.Y.E is facilitating activities between youth and elderly that promote artistic and entrepreneurial collaboration.

Research and Development

Research and Development focuses on product innovation around environmental or social needs, independent of our existing projects.

About

Founded in 2007, our organization exists to create real change through our projects while preparing our members to succeed in their future careers. With a head for business and a heart for the world, we live our values of integrity, passion, innovation and collaboration. Enactus is the world’s largest experiential learning platform dedicated to creating a better world while developing the next generation of entrepreneurial leaders and social innovators.

  • 2007

    Our Humble Beginnings

    Illinois Enactus is founded as a chapter of SIFE - Students in Free Enterprise. SIFE was the world's most successful program helping university students to create community empowerment projects

  • 2012

    Rebranding: Enactus

    SIFE changes its name to Enactus - (En)trepreneurial (Act)ion (Us) - to reaffirm its long-standing commitment to using entrepreneurial action as a catalyst for progress.

  • 2012-2013

    Regional Champions

    Illinois Enactus are Regional Champions in both the 2012 and 2013 Enactus USA Regional Competition.

  • 2014-2015

    National Quarterfinalists

    Illinois Enactus are National Quarterfinalists in both the 2014 and 2015 Enactus USA National Competition.

  • April 2016

    Illinois Enactus reaches the Top 12

    At the 2016 Enactus USA National Competition held in St Louis, Missouri, Illinois Enactus places 3rd in the Semi-Final Round, our best result yet.

  • May 2017

    Illinois Enactus places Top 8

    Illinois Enactus achieves a Top 8 showing in the 2017 Enactus USA National Competition. We presented our projects Grounds for Growth, En-ABLE-ing Entrepreneurs, and Operation Forward.
